Water

Municipal Brief

Paris is hosting the Paralympics in 2024. One of the events at the Paralympics is an open water swimming event in the Seine (apparently the water is clean or so the French say). Since the City of Amsterdam thinks it is beter than Paris, they want to host an event before the Paralympics, snubbing the Parisians. The idea is to host a 5km. open water swimming event through the canals of Amsterdam. You are asked by the municipality of Amsterdam to advise on the feasibility of the event from the perspective of the safety of the partaking athletes from an environmental perspective. The event is going to be hosted in May.


Impact on commercial water transport

Data type: Geopackage
Numerical: Non-numerical
Python suitability: Yes, to read and write for this data type you can install Geopandas library along with Fiona and Shapely
Geometrical type geo-data: Lines

This map shows all commercial water routes within the Netherlands. In Amsterdam, commercial routes inside the city center are limited. The data indicates that the canals within Het IJ, (around) the Prinsengracht, and the Amstel are not designated for commercial boat travel. This implies that within the center of Amsterdam, there is indeed the possibility of hosting a swimming event, without interfering with commercial boat routes.

Official swimming locations

Data type: CSV
Numerical: Non-numerical
Python suitability: Yes, you can use Jupyterlab for reading and writing CSV
Geometrical type geo-data: Points

This map displays the official swimming locations in open water within the municipality of Amsterdam. Waternet, the water management company of the city, recommends using these designated spots for swimming due to various factors, such as heavy rainfall and water transparency, that should be considered when using unapproved locations (Source: Safe Swimming, n.d.). Given the lack of official swimming locations in the center of Amsterdam, further research is needed to determine their suitability for hosting an outdoor swimming event.

Data retrieved from: Gemeente Amsterdam

Peak precipitation

Data type: Metadata
Numerical: Non-numerical
Python suitability: No, metadata is data that provides information about other data, but not the content of the data itself.
Geometrical type geo-data: Points

This map shows locations at a heightened risk of flooding, and as you can see the city center has a lot of locations that are at risk of flooding after heavy rainfall. In these areas, there's a greater probability of sewer overload, which can result in the discharge of polluted water from the sewage system into water bodies. These pollutants can have adverse health effects on swimmers. Therefore Waternet advises against swimming for three days after heavy rainfall (Safe Swimming, n.d.).

Data retrieved from: Klimaateffectatlas

Water transparency

Data type: Unknown
Numerical: Unknown
Python suitability: Unknown
Geometrical type geo-data: Points

This map presents data on water transparency levels in Amsterdam. While this may not directly determine swim safety on its own, water transparency can serve as a critical indicator of swim safety. Clear water with high transparency is generally safer for swimming, as it allows swimmers to detect potential underwater hazards. On the other hand, Waternet advises not to swim in murky water (Safe Swimming, n.d.) , as this water can obscure these obstacles and may be more contaminated. A closer look at the map reveals that the water in the center of Amsterdam is generally quite transparent.

Data retrieved from: AGV

Conclusion

Our analysis suggests that hosting the proposed swimming event in Amsterdam's Grachtengordel area faces significant challenges due to the extensive canal boat routes and mooring points. An alternative, more suitable location is the Jordaan neighborhood, with fewer canal boat activities. Emphasizing water quality and safety, it's advisable to avoid hosting the event for at least three days after heavy rainfall, considering the risk of sewer overload and water contamination in the city center. On a positive note, water transparency in the city center aligns with safe swimming practices. In conclusion, the Jordaan neighborhood emerges as the most practical choice, requiring thorough coordination with authorities and safety assessments, particularly post-rainfall precautions, for a successful event.


Reccomendations

The absence of sufficient available and downloadable data, strongly underscores the need for thorough research before proceeding with the swimming event. To address this data gap, about water quality for example, it is highly advisable to conduct water quality assessments and tests in the heart of Amsterdam. This precautionary measure will help ensure the safety and suitability of the chosen location for participants, considering the potential health and safety risks associated with unapproved swimming spots and uncertain water conditions.
